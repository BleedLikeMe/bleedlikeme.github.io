❓ How to Play

🏠 Overview

You are a racehorse trainer starting with a small string of horses and a modest budget. Buy horses, train them up, enter them in races across the British and Irish calendar, and build your reputation over two seasons. Prize money, training fees and shrewd betting fund your operation.

🏇 Horses

Buying: Purchase horses from the Market (horses for immediate use) or Yearling Sales (cheap, need time to mature). Each horse has a hidden ability rating that determines its racing quality — inspect their form and class records for clues.

Fitness & Health: Fitness (0–100) is spent racing and restored by training. Health decays slightly from racing and can be damaged by injury or vet events; it recovers with rest. A horse at low fitness or health will underperform.

Discipline: Flat horses run on turf/AW in the summer; NH (National Hunt) horses jump hurdles and fences in autumn/winter. A horse must be schooled (Training tab) before it can enter chases or hurdl races.

Ratings: Official Rating (OR) rises with good finishes and drops with poor ones. It affects handicap weights: higher-rated horses carry more weight.

🏃 Training

Assign daily work from the Training tab: Canter (light, steady fitness), Gallop (big fitness boost, more injury risk), Rest (recovery), School hurdles/fences (unlock NH racing). Fitness only grows when a horse works; it drops after every race.

Facilities (Yard tab) reduce injury risk or boost recovery — upgrade when finances allow.

🚌 Racing & Entering

Go to the Racing tab to see upcoming fixtures. Click a race to enter one of your horses. Key factors:

  • Class 1–6: Class 1 is the highest quality (Group/Grade 1). Enter horses in races suited to their ability — outclassed horses rarely place.
  • Race type: Conditions/maiden = level weights. Handicap = weights assigned by OR (better horses carry more). Claiming = horses can be bought by other connections after the race.
  • Distance & Going: Each horse has preferred distance and going. A mismatch costs performance.
  • Jockey: Hire a stable jockey from Staff, or book a freelance jockey when entering. Skill matters, especially in tight finishes.

Races animate in the Race Day view. Your horse's result updates form, fitness and owner satisfaction. Prize money is paid to your balance immediately.

📆 Seasons & Calendar

Flat turf: March – November (Newmarket, Ascot, Epsom, York, Goodwood, Doncaster, etc.).
All-Weather (AW): Year-round at Kempton.
National Hunt: Year-round but peaks October – April (Cheltenham, Aintree, Kempton, Newbury, etc.).

Both codes run simultaneously — you can have flat and jump horses in training at the same time.

Festivals offer much bigger prize pots: Royal Ascot (June), Cheltenham Festival (March), Grand National Meeting (April), Epsom Derby Meeting (June), St Leger Meeting (September), Ayr Gold Cup (September), Ladbrokes Trophy at Newbury (November), King George at Kempton (Boxing Day), Scottish Grand National (April).

🎲 Betting

Place bets from the Betting tab on any upcoming race — including ones where you have a horse entered. Two bet types:

  • Win: Horse must finish first. Full odds returned on stake.
  • Each-way (EW): Half stake on win, half on place (top 2–3 depending on field size). Lower returns but safer.

Odds reflect the simulated market — favourite horses pay less. You can only see odds once the race is on your screen.

👥 Staff

Hire from the Staff tab: Head Lad (boosts daily training gains), Vet (reduces injury risk & vet bills), Jockey (your retained rider — skill affects every race), Conditional (apprentice jockey; earns a weight allowance until 75 winners). Staff cost weekly wages — don't over-hire early on.

📈 Share Exchange

From the Shares tab, buy percentage stakes in NPC horses. When those horses earn prize money, your share of winnings is credited automatically. Listings refresh weekly — prices reflect recent form. You can sell holdings back (at market price) at any time.

💵 Finances

Weekly wages are deducted every Friday. Training fees from owner-horses arrive the same day. Vet bills hit randomly. Watch the Finances tab ledger — going into the red triggers a warning and can spiral quickly if wages aren't managed.

Horses owned by outside clients (non-self) have an owner satisfaction meter. Race them regularly and finish respectably or they'll remove the horse from your yard.

⏩ Fast-Forward & Saving

Advance Day moves one calendar day forward, animating any race meetings today. Fast-forward 1 Week silently simulates 7 days — all races run, results logged, prizes paid, bets settled — then the UI updates. Useful for skipping quiet spells.

Use the 💾 Save button to save to one of four slots (browser localStorage). Your game is also auto-saved after each day.
